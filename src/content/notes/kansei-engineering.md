# 感性工学

Kansei engineering treats subjective experience as an engineering input.

```text
felt experience → designed system
```

Traditional applications ask how a product can be designed to evoke or satisfy a feeling. Q.U.I.L.L. asks a related cybernetic question:

> How should a computational system represent and respond to the operator's affective state so the operator can continue acting and communicating?

This is the intellectual home of `<affective>engineering</tags>`: affect that is parsed, inspectable, routable, and capable of changing system behavior rather than merely decorating a prompt.

See [[Computational Semantics]], [[AAC]], and [[Q.U.I.L.L.]].
